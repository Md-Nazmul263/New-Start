Maulana Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani

1880-1976


The Red Maulana

Bhasani, Maulana Abdul Hamid Khan. Popularly known as Maulana Bhasani, Abdul Hamid Khan was self-educated, village-based, a fire-brand, and skeptical about colonial institutions. Though immensely influential throughout his political career and instrumental in winning many general and local government elections since 1946, he consistently stayed away from holding actual power. His leadership was rooted in his relentless and incessant struggle for safeguarding the rights and interests of the peasantry and the labouring classes.


Early Life and Political Beginnings:

  • Born in Dhangara, Tangail, in British India (now Bangladesh) on December 12, 1880.
  • Participated in the Khilafat Movement, protesting the dissolution of the Ottoman Empire.
  • Led the Muslims of Assam in the 1947 Sylhet Referendum, which resulted in Sylhet joining Pakistan.
  • A graduate of the Deoband seminary, he was a devout Muslim and a strong advocate for Islamic values.

Political Career and Activism:

  • Founded the East Pakistan Peasant Association, gaining widespread popularity among peasants.
  • Co-founded the Awami League, a major political party in Bangladesh, serving as its first president.
  • Later formed the National Awami Party (NAP), advocating for greater autonomy for East Pakistan.
  • Known for his Islamic Socialism, he was sometimes referred to as the "Red Maulana".
  • Played a crucial role in the 1969 Mass Uprising in East Pakistan, which led to the fall of the Ayub Khan regime and the release of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman.
  • Boycotted the 1970 Pakistan general elections, which contributed to the electoral victory of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman.

Legacy

  • Bhashani's political career spanned several decades, marked by his advocacy for the rights of the oppressed, particularly peasants.
  • His strong stance against colonialism and his left-leaning politics made him a significant figure in the region's political landscape.
  • He is remembered for his role in the independence movement of Bangladesh and his dedication to social justice.
  • Maulana Bhashani passed away on November 17, 1976, in Dhaka, Bangladesh.
